Policy Ref: SP-400
Rule: a received shipment that falls well short of what was ordered is a fill-rate disruption and should trigger a follow-up order rather than being silently absorbed into available stock.
Threshold: fill_rate_pct < 70 percent on a single shipment is a disruption (compare to the nominal fill_rate_pct on the supplier's master record, which should sit at 90 percent or higher).
Action: flag the shipment disrupted=true, compute shortfall_units = ordered_qty - received_qty, and route it through RP-200/RP-210 as a follow-up order against the same supplier.

Policy Ref: SP-410
Rule: a delivery that lands far later than its expected date is a lead-time disruption, separate from a fill-rate disruption — the quantity may eventually be correct, but the timing risk needs to be flagged while the order is still in transit.
Threshold: actual_date more than 50 percent later than the gap between ordered_date and expected_date (i.e. the delay alone exceeds half the originally quoted lead_time_days) is a disruption.
Action: flag expedite_required = true for any SKU/store whose on-hand is projected to fall below safety_stock_units (SL-100) before the delayed shipment's actual_date. Do not place a duplicate order while the original is still in transit (RP-200).
Exception: a supplier with reliability_score below 4.0 should be reviewed for an alternate/backup source regardless of whether a specific shipment breached SP-400 or SP-410 this cycle.
